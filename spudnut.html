Spudnuts: the potato
delicacy of the past

Everyday when my grandmother
walked home from school in her
hometwon of El Dorado, Arkansas,
she would stop by the Spudnut
Shoppe with her friend Nancy.
Spudnuts are the potato based
donut that once dominated the
continental US. They are fluffy
and light donut shaped pastries
fried in crisco and coated with
a light glaze.
------------------------------
Spudnut shops, a past American
staple rose to fame in the late
40's. There were over 300 spudnuts
stores across the country. But by
the late 60's the craze was over
and spudnut shops closed their
doors. Less than 30 remain today.
